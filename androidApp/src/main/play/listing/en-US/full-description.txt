Wingmate is a free, open-source augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) app that gives a voice to people who cannot speak. Whether from cerebral palsy, ALS, stroke, autism, or any other condition, Wingmate helps you communicate with natural, high-quality speech — without the high cost of proprietary AAC devices.

WHY WINGMATE?

Most AAC devices and apps are expensive and locked down. Wingmate is different — it's completely free, open source (GPL 3.0), and puts you in control. Instead of paying a monthly subscription for premium voices, you bring your own free Azure key and get access to Microsoft's world-class neural voices. Your data stays private: text prediction runs entirely on-device, and audio can be cached for offline use.

FEATURES

• Natural Azure Neural Voices — Crystal-clear AI voices from Microsoft Azure Cognitive Services. Choose from hundreds of voices across dozens of languages and regions. Adjust pitch and speaking rate to match your personality.

• Bring Your Own Azure Key — Sign up for a free Azure account, create a Speech resource (the F0 free tier gives you 500,000 characters per month at no cost), and enter your endpoint region and subscription key in the app. That's it — premium voices without a premium price.

• Two Communication Modes — Use Keyboard & Phrases to type and save commonly used sentences, or switch to Screen mode for grid-based AAC boards with customizable buttons, images, and navigation.

• Smart Text Prediction — A privacy-first n-gram engine learns your speech patterns and predicts the next word or letter — all on-device. No data ever leaves your phone.

• Save and Organize Phrases — Create categories, color-code buttons, add symbols, and reorder with drag-and-drop. Build your own communication library.

• Import Existing Boards — Supports the Open Board Format (OBF/OBZ), the industry standard for AAC. Import boards from other AAC apps, download starter packs, or create from scratch.

• Open Symbols & ARASAAC — Search and download pictogram symbols from ARASAAC and OpenSymbols to make buttons visual and intuitive. Full offline symbol pack available (~300 MB).

• Pronunciation Dictionary — Override how specific words are spoken using IPA phonemes. Auto-guess pronunciations via Wiktionary. Perfect for names, medical terms, or regional accents.

• SSML Controls — Fine-tune speech with pitch, rate, pauses, and multi-language segments within a single sentence. Shorthand tags like <en>text</en> make it easy.

• Full-Screen Display — Show your message in large text for a communication partner — ideal for face-to-face conversation or external displays.

• Audio Recording — Record custom audio for any saved phrase.

• Offline Backup Voices — Download voices and cache synthesized audio so you can speak even without an internet connection.

• Pronunciation & Symbol Search — Look up IPA pronunciations and find symbols to make each button exactly right.

• Accessibility — High contrast mode, large fonts, dwell-to-select, auditory fishing (whisper label on hover), adjustable tap timing, and customizable UI scaling.

• Board Editor — Full visual editor for screen-based AAC. Resize cells, assign actions (speak, navigate, spell, erase, clear, go home), and lock screens to prevent accidental edits.

SETTING UP AZURE IN WINGMATE

1. Go to portal.azure.com and create a free Azure account.
2. Create a "Speech" resource. Choose the F0 (Free) tier — it includes 500,000 characters of speech per month, more than enough for daily use.
3. Note your resource's region (e.g., "eastus", "westus", "northeurope") and your subscription key (shown in the Azure Portal under "Keys and Endpoint").
4. Open Wingmate, go to Settings → Azure TTS Configuration, and enter your endpoint region and subscription key.
5. Save — Wingmate will fetch the available voices, and you're ready to speak.

PRIVACY

Wingmate is built with privacy at its core. Text prediction is 100% on-device — no cloud, no tracking. Your phrases, board content, and Azure key are stored locally. Optional Firebase Analytics (disabled by default) only collects anonymous feature-interaction events — never your speech, keys, or personal data.
